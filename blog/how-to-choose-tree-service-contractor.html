How to Choose a Tree Service Contractor in Oklahoma

Published March 1, 2026 by Comanche Tree Experts Team

Hiring the right tree service contractor can mean the difference between a safe, professional job and a costly disaster. Tree work is inherently dangerous, and the wrong contractor can damage your property, leave a mess, or worse. Whether you need a single tree removed or ongoing maintenance for your entire property, here is what to look for when choosing a tree service company in Oklahoma.

Verify Licensing and Insurance

This is the most important step and the one most homeowners skip. In Oklahoma, any tree service operating commercially should carry proper business licensing, general liability insurance, and workers' compensation coverage. General liability protects your property if the crew damages your home, fence, or landscaping during the job. Workers' compensation protects you from liability if a worker is injured on your property.

Ask every contractor you consider for proof of insurance before work begins. A reputable company will provide certificates of insurance without hesitation. If a contractor cannot or will not show proof of coverage, move on immediately.

Check Reviews and References

Online reviews are one of the best tools for evaluating a tree service contractor. Check Google Business Profile reviews, Facebook recommendations, and the Better Business Bureau. Look for patterns in reviews rather than focusing on a single positive or negative comment. Consistent praise for professionalism, cleanup quality, fair pricing, and communication are strong indicators of a reliable company.

You can also ask the contractor for references from recent jobs in your area. A company that has been operating in the Lawton area for several years should have no trouble providing local references you can contact.

Get Multiple Written Estimates

Always get at least two or three written estimates before committing to a contractor. A professional tree service will visit your property, assess the work in person, and provide a detailed written quote that includes the scope of work, pricing, timeline, and cleanup details. Be cautious of estimates given over the phone or by text without an on-site visit, as the final price often ends up much higher.

When comparing estimates, do not automatically choose the cheapest option. An unusually low bid often means the contractor is uninsured, inexperienced, or plans to cut corners. Compare the scope of work included in each estimate and weigh price against the company's reputation and credentials.

Ask About Equipment and Safety Practices

Professional tree service requires professional equipment. Ask what equipment the contractor uses and whether their crew follows industry safety standards. Reputable companies use commercial-grade chainsaws, chippers, bucket trucks, and rigging systems. Their crews wear proper personal protective equipment including hard hats, eye protection, hearing protection, and chainsaw chaps.

Be wary of contractors who show up with a pickup truck, a ladder, and a consumer-grade chainsaw. Proper equipment is not just about efficiency. It is about doing the job safely and protecting your property.

Confirm Cleanup and Debris Removal

A complete tree service job includes thorough cleanup. Before signing any agreement, confirm exactly what cleanup is included. Will the contractor haul away all branches, wood, and debris? Is stump grinding included or quoted separately? Will they rake the area and leave your yard clean?

Some contractors quote a low price for the cut but charge extra for cleanup and hauling. Get the full picture upfront so there are no surprises when the work is done. At Comanche Tree Experts, every job includes complete debris removal and site cleanup as part of our standard service.

Ask About Emergency Availability

Oklahoma weather is unpredictable. Severe thunderstorms, tornadoes, and ice storms can drop trees on homes, vehicles, and power lines with little warning. When choosing a tree service contractor, ask whether they offer emergency response and what their typical response time is. A company that provides 24/7 emergency tree service gives you peace of mind knowing you have a trusted professional to call when disaster strikes.

Red Flags to Watch For

Avoid any tree service contractor that displays these warning signs:

  • Door-to-door solicitation: Reputable tree companies do not go door to door looking for work, especially after storms.
  • Demands payment upfront: A professional contractor will not ask for full payment before the job is complete.
  • No written estimate: Verbal quotes are worthless if a dispute arises. Always insist on a written estimate.
  • Cannot provide proof of insurance: This is a non-negotiable requirement. No insurance means no hire.
  • Recommends topping trees: Topping is a harmful, outdated practice that damages tree health. Any company that recommends it lacks proper training.
  • No physical business address or local presence: Fly-by-night operators are difficult to hold accountable if problems arise.
