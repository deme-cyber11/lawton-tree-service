Signs Your Tree Needs a Health Assessment

  • Premature Leaf Drop or Discoloration: Yellowing, browning, or wilting leaves outside of normal seasonal change
  • Bark Abnormalities: Peeling, cracking, or cankers on the trunk and major limbs
  • Fungal Growth: Mushrooms, conks, or powdery mildew on the trunk, roots, or soil line
  • Dieback in the Crown: Dead branches concentrated at the top or tips of the canopy
  • Leaning or Root Heaving: Sudden lean, exposed roots, or soil lifting near the base
  • Insect Activity: Bore holes, sawdust-like frass, or visible pest infestations
  • Reduced Growth: Noticeably smaller leaves, fewer blooms, or stunted new growth compared to previous years

What Our Assessment Includes

A Lawton Tree Pros health assessment is far more than a quick visual inspection. Our arborists evaluate your tree from the root zone to the crown tip using proven diagnostic methods:

  • Full Visual Inspection: Trunk integrity, branch structure, leaf condition, and root collar examination
  • Soil and Root Analysis: Checking for compaction, drainage issues, and root disease
  • Disease and Pest Identification: Pinpointing the exact pathogen or insect responsible for decline
  • Structural Risk Evaluation: Assessing whether the tree poses a safety hazard to people or property
  • Written Diagnostic Report: A clear summary of findings with photographs and supporting data
  • Custom Treatment Plan: Actionable recommendations including timelines, costs, and expected outcomes

Common Tree Health Issues in Oklahoma

Oklahoma's hot summers, unpredictable storms, and clay-heavy soils create a challenging environment for many tree species. Our team regularly diagnoses and treats the following conditions across the Lawton area:

  • Oak Wilt: A lethal fungal disease that spreads rapidly through red oak groups, causing rapid leaf loss and tree death
  • Bacterial Leaf Scorch: A chronic infection common in sycamores and oaks that causes progressive browning along leaf margins
  • Hypoxylon Canker: An opportunistic fungus that attacks drought-stressed oaks, producing dark, crusty patches on the bark
  • Cotton Root Rot: A soil-borne fungus devastating to a wide range of species in southwestern Oklahoma's alkaline soils
  • Emerald Ash Borer and Borers: Wood-boring insects that weaken trees from the inside, often unnoticed until significant damage is done
  • Webworm and Bagworm Infestations: Seasonal pests that defoliate trees and weaken them over successive years

Early detection is critical for most of these conditions. A professional assessment can catch problems before they become untreatable, potentially saving you thousands of dollars in removal costs and preserving mature trees that took decades to grow.
