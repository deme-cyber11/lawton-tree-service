When to Call for Emergency Tree Service

Not every fallen branch requires an emergency call, but certain situations demand immediate professional attention. Contact us right away if you encounter any of the following:

  • Tree on a structure: A tree or large limb has fallen onto your home, garage, fence, or vehicle
  • Blocked roadway or driveway: Fallen trees preventing safe vehicle access or creating traffic hazards
  • Contact with power lines: Any tree or branch touching or near electrical lines (stay clear and call immediately)
  • Leaning or split tree: A tree that has partially uprooted or split and could fall at any moment
  • Hanging limbs: Large broken branches caught in the canopy that could drop without warning
  • Root heave after storms: Soil displacement around the base indicating imminent failure

What to Expect from Our Emergency Response

Safety is our first priority on every emergency call. When our crew arrives, we immediately secure the area and assess the full scope of damage before making any cuts. Storm Damage Cleanup

Oklahoma sits squarely in the heart of severe weather country. Lawton and surrounding communities face tornado season from April through June, powerful straight-line winds during summer supercells, and devastating ice storms that can snap even healthy hardwoods from December through February. After a major weather event, our crews shift into full storm-response mode, triaging emergency calls by severity and working extended hours until every hazardous situation in our queue is resolved.

Our storm damage cleanup services go beyond simply cutting up the tree that fell. We remove all debris from your property, chip branches on site, haul away trunk sections, and rake the work area clean. If the storm revealed additional hazard trees on your property, we'll flag those during our walkthrough and provide a free estimate for preventive removal so you're prepared before the next round of severe weather hits.
