5 Signs You Need Tree Service

March 8, 2026 · 4 min read

Trees are valuable assets to any property, providing shade, beauty, and even increasing home value. But when a tree becomes unhealthy, damaged, or hazardous, it can pose serious risks to your home, vehicles, and family.

As a homeowner in Lawton, OK, it's important to recognize the warning signs that indicate your tree needs professional attention. Here are five critical signs that it's time to call a certified arborist.

1. Dead or Hanging Branches

Dead branches are one of the most obvious and dangerous signs of tree trouble. These branches can fall without warning, especially during Oklahoma's severe thunderstorms and high winds.

What to look for:

  • Branches with no leaves during the growing season
  • Brittle, dry wood that snaps easily
  • Peeling or missing bark
  • Branches hanging at odd angles or partially broken

Dead branches, also called "widow makers," should be removed promptly by a professional tree service. Attempting to remove them yourself can be extremely dangerous, particularly if they're large or near power lines.

2. Leaning or Tilted Trunk

While some trees naturally grow at a slight angle, a sudden or severe lean is a major red flag. A leaning tree often indicates root damage, soil erosion, or structural instability.

When to be concerned:

  • The tree wasn't leaning before but is now
  • The lean is greater than 15 degrees from vertical
  • You see exposed roots or soil heaving on one side
  • The lean is toward a structure, power line, or high-traffic area

Leaning trees in Lawton are often the result of severe weather, particularly after heavy rains that saturate the soil or strong winds from spring storms. If you notice a tree leaning, call a certified arborist immediately for an assessment. This is not a DIY job.

3. Fungus, Mushrooms, or Mold Growth at the Base

If you see mushrooms, fungus, or mold growing at the base of your tree or on the trunk, it's a clear sign of internal decay. Fungi feed on decaying wood, meaning the tree's structural integrity is compromised from the inside out.

Common signs of decay:

  • Mushrooms or shelf fungi (conks) growing on the trunk or roots
  • Soft, spongy bark
  • Cavities or hollow areas in the trunk
  • Sawdust-like material near the base (a sign of insect infestation)

Internal decay weakens the tree and increases the risk of failure during high winds or storms. A certified arborist can assess the extent of decay and recommend whether the tree can be saved with treatment or needs to be removed.

4. Root Damage or Heaving

A tree's roots are its foundation. Damage to the root system can destabilize the entire tree, making it a serious hazard.

Signs of root problems:

  • Exposed roots above ground
  • Cracked or lifting soil around the base
  • Recent construction or excavation near the tree
  • Wilting or discolored leaves despite adequate water

In Lawton, root damage often occurs due to soil erosion from heavy rains, construction activity, or pest infestations. Trees with compromised root systems are unstable and can topple over, particularly during Oklahoma's notorious spring storms.

5. Storm Damage

Oklahoma is no stranger to severe weather. Thunderstorms, tornadoes, ice storms, and high winds can cause significant damage to trees, even if the damage isn't immediately obvious.

Post-storm warning signs:

  • Split or cracked trunks
  • Large branches broken or hanging
  • Bark stripped from the trunk
  • The tree is leaning after a storm
  • Uprooted or partially uprooted trees

Even if a storm-damaged tree looks stable, internal damage may have occurred. After any severe weather event, it's wise to have a professional arborist inspect your trees for hidden damage. Waiting too long can result in sudden tree failure when the next storm hits.

Don't Wait Until It's Too Late

Ignoring these warning signs can lead to costly property damage, personal injury, or even tragedy. If you notice any of these five signs, it's time to call a professional tree service in Lawton, OK.
