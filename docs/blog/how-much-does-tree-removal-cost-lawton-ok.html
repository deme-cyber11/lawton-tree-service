How Much Does Tree Removal Cost in Lawton, OK?

Published March 15, 2026 by Lawton Tree Pros Team

If you have a dead, diseased, or hazardous tree on your Lawton property, one of the first questions you will ask is how much it costs to remove. The answer depends on several factors, but understanding typical pricing in the Lawton area will help you budget appropriately and avoid overpaying.

In this guide, we break down tree removal costs in Lawton, OK for 2026, explain what drives pricing, and share practical tips to get the best value.

Average Tree Removal Costs in Lawton, OK

Tree removal costs in Lawton typically range from $350 to $2,200, with most homeowners paying between $800 and $1,200 for a medium-sized tree. Here is a breakdown by tree size:

Small Trees (Up to 30 Feet) -- $350 to $600

Small trees like ornamental varieties, young crepe myrtles, or small Bradford pears are the most affordable to remove. These jobs typically take 1 to 2 hours and require minimal heavy equipment. Examples common in Lawton include young pecans, redbuds, and small elms that have outgrown their space or developed disease.

Medium Trees (30 to 60 Feet) -- $600 to $1,000

Most mature residential trees in Lawton fall into this category, including red oaks, pecans, American elms, and hackberries. Medium tree removal requires professional-grade equipment and proper safety protocols. These jobs typically take 2 to 4 hours, depending on accessibility and proximity to structures.

Large Trees (60+ Feet) -- $1,000 to $2,200

Large, mature trees such as towering cottonwoods, old-growth pecans, and massive oaks require specialized rigging equipment, sometimes a crane, and a larger crew. These removals can take a full day or longer, especially when the tree is near power lines, buildings, or fences. Many of Lawton's established neighborhoods have trees in this size range that require expert handling.

Factors That Affect Tree Removal Cost

Several variables determine the final price of your tree removal project:

Tree Size and Height

This is the single biggest cost factor. Taller and wider trees require more labor hours, heavier equipment, and larger crews. A 70-foot cottonwood takes significantly more work than a 20-foot ornamental tree. Trunk diameter also matters, as thick hardwood trunks take longer to cut and process.

Location and Accessibility

Trees near power lines, homes, garages, fences, or pools are more complex and dangerous to remove. If the tree is in a tight backyard with no equipment access, the crew may need to climb and rig sections down piece by piece, which adds time and cost. Open lot removals where a tree can be felled in one direction are the least expensive.

Tree Condition

Dead, diseased, or structurally compromised trees are unpredictable and more hazardous to work around. Hollow trunks, split crotches, and hanging dead limbs all require extra precautions and specialized cutting techniques that add to the total cost.

Stump Removal

Most tree removal quotes do not include stump grinding. Expect to pay an additional $75 to $300 per stump, depending on the stump diameter and root structure. Stump grinding is highly recommended to prevent regrowth, eliminate trip hazards, and reclaim usable yard space.

Emergency Removal

Storm-damaged or fallen trees that require same-day or after-hours response typically cost 1.5 to 2 times more than scheduled removals. Oklahoma's severe thunderstorm season from April through June generates significant emergency tree work across the Lawton area each year.

How to Save Money on Tree Removal

Here are practical ways to reduce your tree removal costs without sacrificing quality or safety:

  • Get Multiple Estimates: Compare quotes from at least 2 to 3 licensed, insured tree service companies. Avoid unusually low bids from uninsured operators.
  • Schedule Off-Season: Winter months from December through February are typically slower for tree companies, and some offer discounts during this period.
  • Bundle Services: If you need multiple trees removed, trimmed, or stumps ground, ask for a package price. Most companies offer discounts for bundled work.
  • Keep the Firewood: Ask the crew to leave cut logs instead of hauling them away. This saves disposal costs and gives you free firewood.
  • Address Problems Early: A leaning or diseased tree that can still be safely felled costs less to remove than one that has already fallen on a structure or power line.

Why Hire a Licensed and Insured Tree Service

Tree removal is one of the most dangerous jobs in the country. Hiring an unlicensed or uninsured operator puts your property and finances at serious risk. An uninsured contractor who damages your home or injures themselves on your property can leave you holding the bill. Always verify your tree service is licensed to operate in Oklahoma, carries general liability and workers' compensation insurance, uses professional-grade equipment, and provides a written estimate before starting work.
