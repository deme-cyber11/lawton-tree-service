Professional Stump Grinding Services

After a tree comes down, the stump left behind can be an eyesore, a tripping hazard, and a magnet for pests. Using commercial-grade stump grinding equipment with carbide-tipped cutting wheels, our licensed and insured crew grinds stumps 6 to 12 inches below ground level, leaving your yard clean, level, and ready for new landscaping or fresh sod. Every job includes full cleanup, and most single-stump projects are wrapped up in under an hour.

Why Remove a Tree Stump?

It might seem harmless to leave a stump in the ground, but stumps create real problems that only get worse with time. Here's why Lawton homeowners choose professional stump grinding:

  • Tripping Hazard: Stumps are a liability, especially when grass grows around them and hides the edges from children, guests, and anyone walking your yard
  • Pest Magnet: Decaying wood attracts termites, carpenter ants, and beetles that can eventually migrate to your home or other structures on the property
  • Unwanted Regrowth: Many tree species send up new shoots and suckers from an old stump, creating a recurring maintenance problem that never really goes away
  • Curb Appeal: Old stumps drag down the look of an otherwise well-kept lawn and can reduce your property value
  • Mowing Headaches: Navigating around stumps wastes time on every mow and risks damaging your mower blades on exposed roots
  • Disease Spread: Rotting stumps can harbor fungal infections that spread to healthy trees and plants nearby

Our Stump Grinding Process

Every stump grinding job starts with a free on-site estimate. We measure the stump, check for underground utilities, and discuss your plans for the area. Here's what the process looks like from start to finish:

  • Assessment: We evaluate stump diameter, root spread, and proximity to structures, fences, and utility lines to plan the safest approach
  • Equipment Setup: We position the appropriate grinder for the job and establish a safety perimeter to protect your property and landscaping
  • Grinding: Our heavy-duty machine uses a rotating carbide-tipped cutting wheel to chip away the stump 6 to 12 inches below the soil surface
  • Root Grinding: Major surface roots extending from the stump are ground down as well, eliminating trip hazards and preventing regrowth
  • Cleanup: We rake the work area, backfill the hole, and leave the site level. You can keep the wood chips for mulch or we haul them away at no extra charge

Most residential stumps in Lawton are done in 30 to 60 minutes. Larger stumps or multi-stump jobs may take half a day, but we always finish same-day so you're not left waiting.

Stump Grinding vs. Stump Removal

These two terms get used interchangeably, but they're actually different services with different trade-offs:

  • Stump Grinding uses a machine to shred the stump into wood chips below ground level. It's faster, more affordable (most jobs run $100 to $400), and causes minimal disruption to your lawn. The remaining roots decompose naturally underground over time. This is the method we recommend for the vast majority of residential and commercial properties in Lawton.
  • Full Stump Removal means digging out the entire stump and root ball with heavy equipment. This leaves a large crater that needs backfilling and can damage surrounding landscaping, irrigation lines, and driveways. It costs significantly more and is only necessary when the site is being prepared for new construction or deep excavation.

For most situations, stump grinding delivers the results you need at a fraction of the cost and disruption. We'll walk you through the best option during your free estimate.
