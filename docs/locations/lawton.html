Lawton's Trusted Tree Service Provider

As the county seat of Comanche County and home to nearly 95,000 residents, Lawton is the largest city in southwest Oklahoma and a community where healthy trees make a real difference. Situated adjacent to Fort Sill, one of the U.S. Army's most important installations, Lawton blends military heritage with a thriving civilian community. Lawton Tree Pros has proudly served homeowners, businesses, and property managers throughout Lawton for over 8 years, delivering professional tree care that keeps properties safe, beautiful, and well-maintained.

Lawton's urban canopy features a diverse mix of native and adapted tree species. Post oak and red oak dominate many established neighborhoods, while pecan trees are prized for both shade and their autumn harvest. Cottonwood trees line creeks and low-lying areas across the city, and eastern red cedar and juniper are common throughout the surrounding landscape. Each species requires specific care techniques, and our experienced crew understands the growth habits, disease vulnerabilities, and pruning needs of every tree common to the Lawton area.

Oklahoma weather puts enormous stress on urban trees. Severe spring thunderstorms bring damaging straight-line winds and occasional tornadoes that can snap branches, uproot trees, or send debris crashing into homes and power lines. Winter ice storms coat limbs with heavy ice, causing widespread breakage especially in pecan and red oak trees. Summer drought and extreme heat stress root systems and can leave trees vulnerable to disease and pest infestations. Our team has seen it all and responds quickly to keep Lawton properties safe year-round.

Common Tree Issues in Lawton

Lawton's climate creates specific challenges that property owners should be aware of. Post oaks are susceptible to hypoxylon canker, a fungal disease triggered by drought stress that can kill mature trees within a single season. Pecan trees frequently suffer from scab and webworm infestations that reduce nut production and weaken branches. Cedar and juniper can become overgrown quickly, crowding out other plants and creating fire hazards near structures.

Storm damage is one of the most common reasons Lawton residents call for emergency tree service. After severe weather, cracked limbs may hang in the canopy and fall without warning days or weeks later. Ice-damaged trees often develop structural weaknesses that only become apparent during the next storm. Our crew performs thorough post-storm inspections and removes all hazardous material to protect your family and property.

Root damage from construction, soil compaction, and changes in drainage patterns also affects many Lawton trees, especially in neighborhoods undergoing renovation or new development. A professional tree health assessment can identify these hidden problems before they lead to costly failures.
